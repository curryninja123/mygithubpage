Honors


National Science Bowl 2015



Science Bowl is a competition in which a team of 5 students buzzes in to solve problems in Math, Physics, Biology, Chemistry, Earth Science, Astronomy, and Energy as quickly as possible. In February 2015, my team and I won the Texas A&M Regional Science Bowl. We then proceeded to represent our region at the National Competition in Washington, DC in May. At this competition, we received 7th among about 60 participating schools.

National Physics Bowl



Physics Bowl is a written competition in which students race against time to solve 40 difficult problems in topics including mechanics, electrodynamics, modern physics, and more. In 2015, I was the lead scorer on my team, which placed 3rd nationally. I was also the highest scoring individual in the state. The next year, I was tied for the second highest in both my state and my school, which tied for 1st nationally.

TMSCA State


For the past four years, I have represented my team at the state level for the Texas Math and Science Coaches Association, a competition which tests proficiency in Number Sense, Calculator Applications, General Mathematics, and General Science. In recent years, I led scoring in the areas of Number Sense, Calculator, and General Science. Every one of these years, we received 1st place overall by a noticeable margin.

IBM Watson Scholarship



In April of 2016, I received the Thomas J. Watson Memorial Scholarship from IBM®. I had to go through a rigorous application process in which I enclosed grades, honors, and essays. Along with the financial award, I received a certificate and a letter from IBM.

University of Houston Mathematics


Each January, the University of Houston runs a mathematics competition in which they run tests focused on Algebra, Geometry, Precalculus, Calculus, Physics, Calculator Applications, and Statistics. In 2015 and 2016 I placed 5th and 2nd, respectively, in Calculus out of approximately 150 students.

Academic World Quest



In February 2014, 2015, and 2016, my team won the Regional Academic World Quest competition, a team quiz about current affairs and geography. In 2014 and 2015 I accompanied my team to the national level, where we received 7th and 4th, respectively.

University Interscholastic League



In May 2015, I represented my school in the field of Science in UIL, a state level academics competition. Our science team placed first by a sizeable margin and we won the overall academics prize. The following year, I was on the Science and Number Sense teams, which won 1st and 2nd place, respectively. We also won 2nd place in overall academics that year.

Houston CodeDay


Houston CodeDay is a hackathon for high school and college students. I attended with a team of 3 others and we won the prize for top web application. We programmed a website called Principia in Node.js and Bootstrap. It is essentially a database for teaching mathematical formulas.

National Merit Finalist


In 2015, I took the National Merit Scholarship Qualification test and received a score that was high enough to enter me into the competition for the scholarship. I applied for said scholarship and was selected as one of the National finalists.

Instructables Robotics


In 2013, Instructables offered a contest in which participants had to make an electronic project that was easy to explain to beginners and was very useful at the same time. I created a robot that would test various analog sensors and move to a local minimum in the room. This entry earned me fifth place in the competition and a Nexus 7.

AP Scholarship


For two years in a row (2015, 2016) I have been selected as a National AP Scholar with Distinguisment. Overall, I have taken 15 AP exams with an average score of about 4.8.
